🚨 NEGLIGENCE = 5 ELEMENTS (MUST KNOW ALL)

1. DUTY: Does defendant owe plaintiff a legal duty?
2. BREACH: Did defendant fail to meet reasonable person standard?
3. ACTUAL CAUSATION: "But-for" breach, would injury occur? (No = no liability)
4. PROXIMATE CAUSATION: Was injury FORESEEABLE result of breach? (Palsgraf rule)
5. DAMAGES: Did plaintiff suffer actual harm? (physical, emotional, property)
⚠️ ALL 5 REQUIRED. Missing one = NO LIABILITY

⚖️ INTENTIONAL TORTS (Persons & Property)

TORTS TO PERSONS

  • Assault: Apprehension of contact (no contact needed)
  • Battery: Actual harmful/offensive contact
  • False Imprisonment: Unlawful confinement
  • IIED: Extreme conduct causing emotional distress
  • Defamation: FALSE statement published damaging reputation
  • Invasion of Privacy: Unauthorized intrusion

TORTS TO PROPERTY

  • Trespass to Land: Unauthorized entry onto property
  • Trespass to Chattels: Interfere with personal property (minor damage)
  • Conversion: Take over property (full value recovery)
Conversion > Trespass to Chattels (more serious)

⚖️ NEGLIGENCE DEFENSES

  • Comparative Negligence: Reduce damages by plaintiff's % fault (80% defendant = 80% recovery)
  • Assumption of Risk: Plaintiff knowingly accepted risk (waiver)
  • Statute of Limitations: Deadline to sue (2-3 years typical)
  • Intervening Cause: Third party's unforeseeable act breaks causal chain

⚖️ LANDMARK CASES (EXAM HEAVY)

Palsgraf v. Long Island Railroad: Duty extends ONLY to foreseeable victims. Unforeseeable victim = no liability even if breach occurred. KEY for proximate cause.
May v. Chrysler Group LLC: Manufacturers owe duty to design safe products & warn of hazards. Products liability established.
Bad Frog Brewery v. NYS Liquor Authority: Government can deny license for offensive labels. Commercial speech has limits.

📜 COURT PROCEDURES (Phases)

  1. Pleadings: Complaint (plaintiff) → Answer (defendant) [establishes claims/defenses]
  2. Discovery: Documents, depositions, interrogatories [exchange information]
  3. Motions: Summary judgment, dismissal [procedural rulings]
  4. Trial: Jury/judge hears evidence [determines liability & damages]
  5. Appeal: Appellate court reviews legal issues [affirm, reverse, or remand]

💼 BUSINESS ETHICS FRAMEWORKS

  • Utilitarianism: Maximize benefit for greatest number
  • Deontological: Follow duties/rules (don't lie, keep promises)
  • Virtue Ethics: Develop good character (honesty, integrity)
  • Justice/Fairness: Treat people equitably

📜 CONSTITUTIONAL LAW (Commerce Clause)

  • Commerce Clause: Congress can regulate interstate commerce & activities substantially affecting it
  • Commercial Speech: Protected but regulated (no false/misleading, can require warnings)
  • Due Process (5th Amendment): Government regs must be rationally related to legitimate interest
  • Key Agencies: FDA (food/drugs), FTC (unfair practices), SEC (securities), OSHA (workplace safety), EPA (environment)
