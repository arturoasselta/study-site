Argument Essay Structure
SectionWhat to Include
IntroHook → context → clear, arguable thesis (takes a position)
Body ¶ 1–3Topic sentence → evidence → commentary → link to thesis
Concession ¶Acknowledge counterargument → refute with stronger evidence
ConclusionRestate thesis (new language) → broaden significance → no new claims
A strong thesis: specific, arguable, and forecasts your reasoning. Avoid "I believe" — state it as fact.
⚠️ Commentary > evidence. Never let a quote "speak for itself" — always analyze HOW it proves your claim.
Synthesis Essay Rules
  • Must cite at least 3 of 6–7 provided sources
  • Cite as: (Source A), (Source B), etc. — no footnotes needed
  • Don't just summarize sources — use them as evidence for YOUR argument
  • Take a clear position in your thesis — synthesis essay IS an argument
  • Sources can agree, disagree, or nuance each other — show the conversation
  • Quotations must be introduced and followed by analysis
Best approach: read all sources → form your position → select best 3-4 that support you → outline → write
AP Exam Strategy & Passage Analysis Tips
Multiple Choice (MCQ)
  • Read questions BEFORE the passage
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
  • Line-reference questions → re-read 5 lines above & below
  • "Purpose" Q's → think about audience + situation
  • "Effect" Q's → think HOW, not just WHAT
Free Response (FRQ)
  • Read prompt twice — underline the verb (analyze, argue, synthesize)
  • Spend 5 min planning before writing
  • Each body ¶: claim → evidence → analysis
  • Score high by explaining HOW rhetorical choices achieve purpose
  • Time: ~40 min per essay
Rhetorical Analysis Essay
  • Thesis must identify specific rhetorical choices
  • Don't list devices — explain WHY writer uses them
  • Connect every device to speaker's purpose
  • Avoid plot summary — analyze, don't narrate
  • Strong verbs: asserts, challenges, underscores, juxtaposes, invokes

Central ThemeIndividual conscience vs. societal norms; freedom vs. oppression
SatireTwain uses Huck's naïve narration to mock racism, religion, "civilization"
JimMore moral than "civilized" characters; challenges white supremacist assumptions

Huck's Moral Growth"All right, then, I'll go to hell" — chooses personal morality over social law

Those Who StayAccept the moral compromise; benefit from the system
Those Who Walk AwayRefuse to participate; reject the utilitarian bargain
